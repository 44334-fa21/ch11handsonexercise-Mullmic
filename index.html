Five Helpful Facts About Copyright Law and the Web

Fact 1

Copyright work made after January 1, 1978, lasts 70 years after the author passes.

Fact 2

Joint work made by two or more authors causes the copyright to stay in effect until the last author dies and 70 years after.

Fact 3

Anyone interested in a copyright may at any time record a statement of the date of death of the author of the copyrighted work.

Fact 4

Copyright in work created before January 1, 1978, but not in the public domain, subsists from January 1, 1978, and endures for the term by section 302.

Fact 5

Any work copyrighted by a corporate body shall be entitled to a renewal and extension of the copyright.
